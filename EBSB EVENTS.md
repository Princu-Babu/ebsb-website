### **Rakhi Making Workshop – Har Ghar Tiranga**

07-08-2025

As part of the Har Ghar Tiranga initiative, the EBSB Club IIT Jammu organized a Rakhi Making Workshop to promote patriotism, unity, and gratitude towards the armed forces. Students enthusiastically participated in crafting handmade rakhis symbolizing respect, appreciation, and emotional connection with the soldiers safeguarding the nation at the borders.

The workshop was conducted in collaboration with Kritash Club, through which the handmade rakhis were planned to be taken to border areas and tied to soldiers as a gesture of solidarity and national pride. The event reflected the spirit of unity, cultural values, and collective respect for the nation’s defenders while encouraging creativity and meaningful participation among students.

**Wall of Evil – Burn the Negativity** 

02-10-2025

### **Wall of Evil**

The “Wall of Evil” activity was organized during Dussehra to symbolize the victory of good over evil and to spread awareness about modern-day social negativities. Inspired by the ten-headed Ravana, the installation represented various societal evils such as anger, greed, jealousy, corruption, discrimination, fear, hatred, and pollution.

A large interactive “Wall of Evil” poster was created near Egret Hostel, where participants wrote additional social evils and negative thoughts on slips and pasted them onto the wall. The activity encouraged collective reflection on overcoming negativity through unity and social responsibility.

The event concluded with a symbolic burning ritual representing the destruction of evil and the triumph of righteousness, inspired by the diverse Dussehra traditions celebrated across India. A reflection and pledge session was also conducted, where students collectively pledged to spread positivity, harmony, and goodness within society.

**Gandhi–Shastri Jayanthi**

**02-10-2025**

On the occasion of the birth anniversaries of Mahatma Gandhi and Lal Bahadur Shastri, a group discussion session was organized in collaboration with students from Law School Jammu focusing on the impact of the Official Language Act, 1963 in promoting unity in diversity in India. Participants explored the historical context and significance of the Official Language Amendment Bill introduced by Lal Bahadur Shastri and discussed its influence on education, governance, culture, and linguistic harmony in modern India.

As part of the session, students referred to selected parliamentary proceedings and engaged in discussions on the advantages, challenges, and future implications of India’s official language policies in a multilingual society. The event encouraged constitutional awareness, critical thinking, and public speaking while aligning with the nationwide celebration of “Hamara Samvidhan, Hamara Swabhimaan” commemorating 75 years of the Constitution of India.

**Tribal Cuisine Unity Carnival** 

31-10-2025

### **Tribal Cuisine Unity Carnival**

The Tribal Cuisine Unity Carnival was organized on the occasion of National Unity Day as part of Janjatiya Gaurav Varsh 2025, commemorating the 150th birth anniversary of Birsa Munda. Organized by the EBSB Club in collaboration with Food Tinkers Club IIT Jammu, the event celebrated the rich tribal heritage, culinary traditions, and cultural diversity of India through food, storytelling, and cultural exchange.

Participants represented different states and tribal communities by preparing authentic tribal dishes and sharing their cultural significance, traditional preparation methods, and nutritional value. The event promoted awareness about sustainable and healthy indigenous food practices while reflecting the EBSB vision of unity in diversity.

### **Suan Kheer**

Suan Kheer is a traditional tribal dessert prepared using indigenous grains and milk, known for its nutritional richness and cultural significance. The dish reflects the use of locally grown grains in tribal communities and highlights traditional methods of preparing healthy festive sweets.

### **Nachani Bhakar with Pithala / Bambusa Arundinacea**

Nachani Bhakar is a nutritious millet roti served with Pithala, a protein-rich gram flour preparation, or Bambusa Arundinacea, a bamboo shoot-based tribal delicacy. The dish reflects the balanced and wholesome dietary traditions followed in tribal regions using locally available ingredients.

### **Eromba**

Eromba is a traditional spicy mashed vegetable preparation from the tribal communities of Northeast India. Prepared using boiled vegetables, local herbs, and spices, the dish showcases indigenous cooking styles and the distinct flavors of northeastern tribal cuisine.

### **Dhuska**

Dhuska is a traditional rice-and-lentil-based tribal delicacy commonly prepared in eastern tribal regions of India. The dish reflects local culinary techniques and is valued for its simplicity, taste, and nutritional content.

**National Voter's Day 2026**   
25-01-2026

National Voters' Day was observed through an innovative hands-on event titled “Arduino-Based EVM Making Challenge: Democracy in Action,” organized by the EBSB Club to promote democratic awareness, responsible citizenship, and technical learning among students.

The event focused on designing and programming functional Electronic Voting Machine (EVM) prototypes using Arduino UNO, enabling participants to understand the practical working of India’s electoral system. Students implemented simplified versions of different Indian election methods, including General Elections using the First Past the Post system, Rajya Sabha Preferential Voting, Vice Presidential Elimination Voting, and the Presidential Election system based on the Single Transferable Vote method.

Technical components, hardware support, and innovation facilities for the challenge were provided with support from Indian Institute of Entrepreneurship Development Cell (I2EDC). By combining electronics, programming, and civic education, the event encouraged experiential learning while helping students understand how democratic institutions function through logical algorithms and hardware systems.

The challenge promoted awareness about voting, constitutional values, democratic participation, and responsible citizenship while reinforcing the EBSB vision of unity in diversity through India’s shared democratic framework.

The Spring New Year celebrations were organized under the Ek Bharat Shreshtha Bharat initiative to highlight the diverse New Year traditions celebrated across India, including Ugadi and Gudi Padwa. Through cultural activities, traditional practices, and student participation, the event promoted cultural exchange, unity in diversity, and appreciation of India’s rich regional heritage.

### **Ethnic Day**

An Ethnic Day was organized as part of the Spring New Year celebrations, where students attended classes dressed in traditional attire representing different regions of India. The cultural and historical significance of hoisting the Gudi was explained to participants, creating awareness about the traditions and festive practices associated with Maharashtra while promoting intercultural understanding and celebration of regional diversity.
