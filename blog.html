EBSB IIT Jammu 2025–26: Celebrating Unity in Diversity

The Ek Bharat Shreshtha Bharat Club successfully organized a vibrant series of cultural, educational, artistic, and socially engaging events throughout the academic year 2025–26, celebrating the spirit of "Unity in Diversity" across the IIT Jammu community. Beginning with the Har Ghar Tiranga Rakhi Making Workshop in August, where students crafted handmade rakhis for soldiers in collaboration with Kritash Club, the journey continued with Janmashtami celebrations featuring Dahi Handi, bhajans, storytelling, and cultural performances showcasing the diverse representations of Krishna across Indian traditions. September witnessed the colourful celebration of Onam – Unity in Bloom through Pookkalam, multilingual discussions, and cultural exchange sessions, followed by Durgashtami celebrations highlighting the artistic and cultural significance of Goddess Durga across India. Gandhi–Shastri Jayanthi and the Wall of Evil initiative during Dussehra encouraged constitutional awareness, social reflection, and discussions on unity and language diversity in collaboration with Law School Jammu students. Diwali celebrations illuminated the campus with more than 14,000 diyas through IIT Jammu and EBSB-themed sketches, engineering-inspired diya workshops, innovative rangoli competitions, and campus-wide diya displays symbolizing collective harmony and creativity. On National Unity Day, the Tribal Cuisine Unity Carnival celebrated indigenous culinary traditions and the legacy of Birsa Munda through authentic tribal dishes and cultural storytelling. The year further included Guru Nanak Jayanti reflection sessions, Constitution Day awareness activities, National Youth Day screenings and discussions inspired by Chaar Sahibzade and Swami Vivekananda, harvest festival celebrations including Lohri, Pongal, and Kite Festival activities, Vasant Panchami and Parakram Diwas cultural programmes, the Arduino-based EVM Making Challenge on National Voters’ Day, the Draw the Immortal art competition inspired by regional depictions of Shiva, eco-friendly Holi celebrations, Ethnic Day cultural interactions, Ugadi and Gudi Padwa festivities, and finally Ambedkar Jayanthi, Vishu, and Puthandu celebrations promoting constitutional values and regional cultural traditions. Through these diverse initiatives, the EBSB Club fostered cultural appreciation, creativity, patriotism, inclusivity, linguistic diversity, and national integration, strengthening the vision of Ek Bharat Shreshtha Bharat within the IIT Jammu community.

Event Highlights & Articles

Har Ghar Tiranga – Rakhi Making Workshop

A Rakhi Making Workshop was organized under the Har Ghar Tiranga initiative in collaboration with Kritash Club. Students handcrafted rakhis for soldiers guarding the nation’s borders, promoting patriotism, gratitude, and unity through creative participation.

Janmashtami & Dahi Handi

Krishna Janmashtami celebrations included Dahi Handi, cultural performances, bhajans, storytelling, and poetry inspired by the diverse literary and cultural representations of Krishna across India, promoting teamwork and cultural exchange.

Onam – Unity in Bloom

Onam was celebrated through Pookkalam, multilingual cultural discussions, songs, and traditional food experiences, promoting linguistic diversity and cultural harmony among students.

Durgashtami

Durgashtami celebrations featured cultural performances highlighting the various regional traditions associated with Goddess Durga and the message of courage, resilience, and unity in diversity.

Gandhi–Shastri Jayanthi & Wall of Evil

A discussion session on the Official Language Act, 1963 was conducted in collaboration with Law School Jammu alongside the interactive "Wall of Evil" activity symbolizing the victory of positivity over social evils during Dussehra celebrations.

Diya Crafting & Designing Workshop

An engineering-themed diya crafting and decorating workshop encouraged students to blend Indian art traditions with scientific and technical concepts through innovative diya designs and creative expression.

Engineering-Themed Rangoli Competition

Students designed rangolis inspired by engineering concepts such as circuits, algorithms, mechanical systems, and scientific structures, creatively combining technical imagination with festive traditions.

Diwali – Radiance in Unity

Diwali celebrations illuminated the IIT Jammu campus with more than 14,000 diyas, including large IIT Jammu and EBSB-themed sketches, symbolizing enlightenment, togetherness, and cultural unity.

Tribal Cuisine Unity Carnival

Organized on National Unity Day as part of Janjatiya Gaurav Varsh, the Tribal Cuisine Unity Carnival celebrated indigenous food traditions and the legacy of Birsa Munda through authentic tribal dishes and cultural storytelling.

Guru Nanak Jayanti

Guru Nanak Gurpurab celebrations included reflections on Sikh teachings, cultural performances, poetry, and discussions promoting equality, service, peace, and cultural harmony.

National Constitution Day

Constitution Day activities promoted awareness about constitutional values, democratic responsibility, and national unity through educational and interactive sessions inspired by the Constitution of India.

National Youth Day

An animated screening of Chaar Sahibzade followed by peer learning discussions encouraged students to reflect on courage, sacrifice, leadership, and the teachings of Swami Vivekananda.

Kite Festival – Uttarayan

A vibrant Kite Festival was organized as part of the Sankranti celebrations to represent the spirit of Uttarayan and harvest festivals celebrated across India. Students enthusiastically participated in kite flying activities symbolizing joy, hope, freedom, and new beginnings.

Bhogi–Lohri Celebration

A harvest festival celebration featuring bonfires, music, and cultural jamming sessions highlighted the diverse harvest traditions celebrated across northern and northeastern India.

Pongal Making Competition

A Pongal-making competition showcased traditional harvest cuisine and encouraged cultural exchange by connecting regional food traditions associated with Sankranti celebrations across India.

Vasant Panchami & Parakram Diwas

Cultural performances, poetry, storytelling, and patriotic reflections celebrated Vasant Panchami alongside the birth anniversary of Subhas Chandra Bose, promoting creativity, patriotism, and cultural harmony.

National Voters’ Day – EVM Challenge

Students designed Arduino-based Electronic Voting Machine prototypes to understand India’s electoral systems and democratic processes through hands-on technical learning and civic awareness.

Draw the Immortal

A cultural art competition organized during Maha Shivaratri encouraged students to artistically represent Shiva through regional Indian art traditions, promoting creativity and cultural appreciation.

Holi – Unity in Every Hue

Eco-friendly Holi celebrations featuring Holika Dahan and Rangwali Holi promoted unity, inclusivity, cultural exchange, and safe festive participation among students.

Ethnic Day

Students attended classes in traditional attire representing different Indian cultures, creating a vibrant campus atmosphere celebrating India’s diversity and shared cultural identity.

Ugadi & Gudi Padwa

Spring New Year celebrations included Ugadi Pachadi distribution, cultural discussions, and the installation of a traditional Gudi, highlighting the regional traditions and philosophies associated with new beginnings.

Ambedkar Jayanthi, Vishu & Puthandu

Celebrations honoring B. R. Ambedkar were conducted alongside Vishu and Puthandu cultural activities, promoting constitutional values, cultural awareness, and the shared spirit of renewal and inclusivity across India.
